Privacy

This page describes what data is collected when you submit a card via pin.html, where it is stored, and how to request its removal. Plain language, no dark patterns.

What we collect

When you submit a card, the following fields are stored in a private Google Sheet:

  • The display name you choose (or anonymous if you leave it empty)
  • The category you select
  • The purpose you describe (free text, 30–250 characters)
  • The technical configuration of your card: app name, title/body keywords and operators, action type (e.g. ringtone, smart device)
  • Your explicit consent flag (always true for accepted submissions)
  • A timestamp generated by the server at the moment your submission is received

What we don't collect

  • No email address, no phone number, no name unless you put one in the display name field
  • No IP address is logged persistently — Google's transport-layer logs are out of our control but are not retained or used by us
  • No browser fingerprint, no device ID, no analytics, no tracking pixels
  • No cookies are set by this site
  • No device-specific data from your card is transmitted: ringtone URIs, audio paths, smart device URLs, your installation ID and the card's internal name are all stripped in your browser before submission

Where the data lives

Submissions are written to a Google Sheet owned by the Sentinel maintainer (br1greenhouse). The sheet is private and is not shared with anyone else. It is used as a review queue to evaluate which cards get published on the public Wall.

How long we keep it

Submissions are kept indefinitely as an audit trail of the consent you provided. Rejected submissions are not deleted physically: their status is set to rejected and they remain in the sheet for accountability.

Removing your data

You can request the deletion of a specific submission by emailing beserra@tiscali.it. Include the display name and the approximate date of submission. Removal is processed manually within a few days.

No tracking

This site (pin.html, privacy.html, and the Wall itself) does not use cookies, local storage, fingerprinting or third-party analytics. The only outbound network call is the submission POST to a Google Apps Script endpoint, and only when you click Submit for review.
